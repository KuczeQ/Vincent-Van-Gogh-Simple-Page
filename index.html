About Van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh was a renowned Dutch painter who is widely considered one of the most influential artists in Western art history. He was born on March 30, 1853, in Zundert, Netherlands, and lived a relatively short life, passing away on July 29, 1890, in Auvers-sur-Oise, France, at the age of 37.

History of Vincent van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh, one of the most renowned and influential artists in history, was born on March 30, 1853, in Zundert, a small village in the Netherlands. He came from a family of art dealers, and although his early life was filled with struggles and setbacks, his artistic journey would ultimately leave an indelible mark on the world.

Van Gogh's passion for art developed during his youth, but he initially pursued a career in various fields such as teaching and working at an art dealership. In 1880, at the age of 27, he decided to dedicate himself fully to art. He began experimenting with different styles and techniques, including his iconic bold brushwork and vivid use of color.
